	<p>Information & Communication Technology<br/>
		(ICT) is another/extensional term for information technology (IT) which stresses the role of unified communications[1] and the integration of telecommunications (telephone lines and wireless signals), computers as well as necessary enterprise software, middleware, storage, and audio-visual systems, which enable users to access, store, transmit, and manipulate information.</p><br/>
		<br/>
		<h4>MORE ARTICLES</h4><br/>
		<p>The term ICT is also used to refer to the convergence of audio-visual and telephone networks with computer networks through a single cabling or link system.</p>
		<p>The broadness of ICT covers any product that will store, retrieve, manipulate, transmit or receive information electronically in a digital form, e.g. personal computers, digital television, email, robots. For clarity, Zuppo provided an ICT hierarchy where all levels of the hierarchy "contain some degree of commonality in that they are related to technologies that facilitate the transfer of information and various types of electronically mediated communications".</p>
